Master's Dissertation

You can download my Master's dissertation here. I wrote about how the GFC altered Leaving Cert (the Irish national entrance exam to third-level education) candidates' choice of majors. I found a significant gender gap in STEM subjects and varied trends in student participation. There was an increase in students taking Agricultural Science, Applied Mathematics, Biology, and Chemistry, but a decrease in those taking Engineering, Mathematics, Physics, and Physics and Chemistry. Additionally, admission points for STEM-related college courses, especially in Healthcare, Chemistry, Nanotechnology, Biology, and Computer Science, saw a notable rise.

Applying Kuhnâ€™s Concept of Scientific Revolutions to the History of Wave-Particle Duality

In this essay, I explore the nature of light and its historical discussions, emphasizing the importance of studying both philosophy and the history of science. I highlight Thomas Kuhn's contributions to understanding how scientific communities function and apply aspects of scientific revolutions to the wave-particle duality period. In the end, I agree with Kuhn's notion that significant scientific progress often requires a commitment that risks being wrong. Despite the uncertainties faced by scientists in the past, the study of light's dual nature has led to remarkable technological advancements. I suggest that even if humanity still doesn't fully understand light, the benefits derived from its study make the pursuit worthwhile.
